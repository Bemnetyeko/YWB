What is it?

Boston University states that social wellbeing is building and maintaining healthy relationships and having meaningful interactions with other people. It involves the connections we have with the people around us and how we communicate.

How to achieve it

Some tips to improve your social wellbeing as stated by NIH are:

  • Make healthy connections
  • Take care of yourself whle taking care of others
  • Bond with your friends and family
  • Get active with others
  • Build strong relationships

Fun Facts!

Here are some fun facts about social wellbeing and the effects it has on us from EW-SO

  • Cholesterol levels go up when human companionship is lacking
  • Socially isolated people are more susceptible to illness and have a death rate two to three times higher than those who are not socially isolated.
  • People who maintain their social network and support systems do better under stress.
